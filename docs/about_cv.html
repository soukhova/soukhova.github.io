About Me

I am a geographer and transportation researcher working at the intersection of urban mobility, social equity and changing climate. My work focuses on understanding how everyday travel patterns shape unequal exposure to environmental risks and access to opportunities, and on developing mobility-aware spatial analytics that can inform more equitable urban systems.

Methodologically, I specialize in R-based, reproducible analytical workflows, GIS, and applied spatial analysis. I have experience designing and maintaining research data packages, contributing analytical functions and pipelines, and integrating large‑scale mobility and environmental datasets into transparent, extensible frameworks. Across projects, I aim to translate rigorous spatial methods into tools and evidence that are both scientifically robust and policy‑relevant.
